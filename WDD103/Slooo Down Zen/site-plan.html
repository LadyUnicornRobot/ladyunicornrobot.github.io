Overview

Purpose

What does it mean to live mindfully? How does one develop that skill? Slooo Down Zen provides bite-size theraputic tools that fit inside a busy life. Our library of vocal, tactile, and mental anchoring practices facilitates tranquility and is easily accessible. Subscribe to the Daily Practice text to help soothe the mind, release anxiety, and Slooo Down.

Audience

My audience will probably be young to middle-aged adults who have careers and/or children. They are people who are already pretty self-aware and who want to achieve a little more peace in their personal lives through mastering of Self. They take the initiative and love to learn and try new things. They will most likely try out the different activities and then teach them to their children, friends, and loved ones to help them improve their quality of life. Others who may be drawn to the site are people who need a little extra help to supplement professional therapy, or people who can’t afford to see a professional, but still need to be guided through developing theraputic tools.

Typography

Heading Font: Karma

It's familiar and easy to see. Readers won't have to mentally sort through a 'busy' font. They will be able to get the message quickly and clearly.

Paragraph Font: Raleway

This is a nice sans-serif font that feels open and clean. It is perfect for easy readability. Readers experience will be clean and simple as they learn about being mindful.

Daily Practice

Set up: Open the mouth, tongue to back of top teeth in an n position.
Practice: Breathe in slowly through the nose. Relax the base of the throat. Breathe out through the nose. Rest in that space. Let the back of your brain float in the quiet dark. Breathe in through the nose. Rest in hang time for a moment. Open the throat and hum a note that is comfortable and low, with the n sound. Keep the note steady. Notice every time it fluxuates. Hum to the end of your breath, but not to discomfort. Repeat this practice several times.
