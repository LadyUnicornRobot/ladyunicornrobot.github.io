Overview

Purpose

The primary purpose for this site is to draw customers. It needs to be eye-catching and easy to understand and navigate. It will showcase beautiful photos. It will clearly inform customers about different rafting experiences and pricing. It will provide a quick and easy place for customers to book and pay for a rafting experience. It will inform customers of company policies and incentives. It will link to social media platforms, especially Instagram and Facebook, in order to create a user experience community where people can share their photos and experiences. It will initiate a personal bond between customers and company employees before they’ve even met, by having a page for employee photos and bios.

Audience

The target customers are young people in their late teens to early 30s who are still in school so they have the summers off. They usually have good enough jobs to pay for fun activities without the demands of a full-time career or family. They are interested in the adrenaline rush that comes from rugged, outdoorsy activities like Jeeping, rock climbing, long-distance running, hiking, and snowboarding. They want to have fun and make memories with their friends; and they also want to capture those memories so they can share them to their social media friends and followers. Target customers are thrill-seekers, so their needs are to constantly have a variety of new experiences. The majority of customers will access this site on their mobile phones.

Typography

Heading Font: Montserrat Light 300

I chose this for the heading because it is crisp and clean. The line of the curved letters matches the lines in the logo and evokes the idea of water. The sharp points on letters like A and K evokes mountain peaks, trees, and the other scenery one would find on a rafting trip. It is a lighter font than the body font, creating a visual experience on the site parallel to the physical one someone would have while rafting under a clear, blue sky.

Paragraph Font: Open Sans Regular 400

I chose this for the paragraph text because it has similar lines to the header style, but is more bold and the letters are closer together, adding contrast. It's a clean, sans serif font for easy reading on a mobile device.

The Site Map of a site is just like it sounds…it is a map of the pages in a site and how they are related and linked together. From the map above we can see that we will eventually have the Home page and 2 sub or child pages.

The lines that connect them all together indicate that each page should be accessible from any other page, it is essentially showing us the global navigation for the site.

Wireframes

Wireframes are like blueprints for making webpages. They should show the major sections of content that will be on the page and the relative locations of each element. In the wireframe below you can see there will be 6 sections to our page:

  1. At the top we have a section with the logo (the box with the mountain means an image) and the navigation bar.
  2. Then there is a banner image that stretches all the way across the screen.
  3. Next we have some text and an image
  4. ...followed by another row made up of an image and some text.
  5. Then one more section of text with no image.
  6. Lastly, a footer containing a copyright/name line and 3 social media icons.
